## Validity Pattern: C5 vs C3 vs C6

### Validity combinations (C5 = left-eye-only mode)

| Condition | Both valid | L valid, R invalid | L invalid, R valid | Both invalid |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| C5 (left eye mode, 120 Hz) | 16,957 | 118 | **743** | 184 |
| C3 proxy (right eye mode, 120 Hz) | 16,957 | 118 | **—** | — |
| C6 (left eye mode, 300 Hz) | — | — | **286** | — |

Critical column: **L invalid, R valid** — samples where the *active* eye (left in C5)
loses tracking while the *discarded* eye (right) remains valid.

### GT labels for the 743 C5 problematic samples (left invalid, right valid)

| GT label | Count |
|---------|-------|
| Fixation | 610 |
| Saccade | 120 |
| Unclassified | 13 |
| EyesNotFound | 0 |

The GT (generated by Tobii Pro Lab) classifies all 743 samples as Fixation or Saccade.
Tobii uses internal eye offset interpolation to estimate the left-eye position from the
right-eye position during these periods, allowing classification to continue.  
Our reconstruction in left-eye-only mode marks these samples as `EyesNotFound`.

This mismatch accounts for **743 / 970 = 76.6 %** of all C5 disagreements.

### Temporal concentration of the 743 problematic samples

| Time segment | Samples | Share |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| First third | 700 | 94.2 % |
| Middle third | 30 | 4.0 % |
| Last third | 13 | 1.7 % |

This closely mirrors the 80.2 % first-third concentration of disagreements.

### Left-eye invalid run structure

- Number of invalid runs: 65
- Mean run length: 14.3 samples (119 ms at 120 Hz)
- Median run length: 1 sample
- **Longest run: 631 samples (5,258 ms ≈ 5.25 s)**

The longest run (631 samples) lies in the first third of the recording and accounts for
631 / 743 = 84.9 % of the left-invalid-right-valid samples.  A single five-second tracking
loss of the left eye, during which the right eye continued tracking, explains the bulk of
the C5 disagreement.

### C3 proxy comparison

For C3 (right-eye mode on the same recording), the problematic samples are those where
the *right* eye is invalid but the *left* eye is valid (118 samples, GT: 73 Fixation, 42
Saccade, 3 Unclassified). This is 6.3× fewer than C5's 743 samples, which is consistent
with C3's higher agreement (κ=0.937 vs κ=0.797).

### C6: why κ=0.998 despite left-eye invalids

The 300 Hz recording (`left20ms30_extracted.tsv`) has 286 samples where the left eye is
invalid and the right eye is valid.  Unlike C5, these 286 samples have **GT = EyesNotFound**
in the ground truth file.  The Tobii ground truth for C6 was therefore exported in a mode
that does *not* fall back to the right eye — instead it marks left-invalid periods as
EyesNotFound.  Our filter does the same, so the two agree perfectly on these samples.

The fundamental difference between C5 and C6 is **how the Tobii ground truth was generated**:
- C5 GT: generated in a mode with right-eye fallback (average or fallback mode)
- C6 GT: generated in a strict left-eye-only mode

## Eye Offset Analysis

Eye offset analysis requires binocular periods (both eyes valid simultaneously).  
For the left-eye-only and right-eye-only configurations, the pipeline does not expose a
stored δ* offset value in the output columns.

C5 recording: 16,957 simultaneously binocular samples (both eyes valid).  
Direct analysis of `(gaze_right - gaze_left)` over these samples would yield the offset
distribution, but this was not computed as the offset interpolation is disabled in the
evaluation configuration for C5.

## Conclusion

**Hypothesis: C5 degradation is caused by a ground truth generation mode mismatch.**

**Evidence strength: Strong.**

The data fully supports this explanation:

1. 743 samples with left-eye tracking loss and right-eye continuation account for 76.6 %
   of C5's 970 disagreements.
2. These samples carry GT labels Fixation or Saccade (Tobii used right-eye fallback in GT
   generation) while our filter produces EyesNotFound.
3. The temporal concentration (94.2 % in first third) matches a single 631-sample invalid
   period that dominates the disagreement count.
4. C3 proxy has only 118 analogous samples (right-invalid, left-valid), explaining its
   higher κ=0.937.
5. C6 has no such mismatch because its GT marks invalid-left periods as EyesNotFound,
   matching our filter's output exactly.

The anomaly is **not** caused by:
- Eye offset interpolation accuracy (the reconstruction does not apply offset interpolation
  in the C5 evaluation configuration)
- Velocity estimation errors near the threshold
- Sampling frequency effects (both C5 and C3 use the same 120 Hz recording)

The root cause is the difference in GT generation: Tobii Pro Lab exported the C5 ground
truth using a fallback mechanism that silently switched to the right eye when the left eye
was lost.
