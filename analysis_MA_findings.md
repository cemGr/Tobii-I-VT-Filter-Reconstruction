## Velocity Signal Comparison

### Critical finding: smoothing is a no-op for `tobii_gaze_dir`

The reconstruction uses the `tobii_gaze_dir` velocity method.  This method computes
angular velocity from **DACS normalised gaze direction vectors** (`gaze_dir_left_x/y/z`,
`gaze_dir_right_x/y/z`) — see `ivt_filter/strategies/velocity_calculation.py:263`
(`TobiiGazeDirAngle`) and `ivt_filter/processing/velocity_samples.py:396–418`.

The noise reduction stage (`ivt_filter/preprocessing/noise_reduction.py`) smooths only
`combined_x_mm` and `combined_y_mm` (screen-plane gaze coordinates).  The direction
vectors used by `tobii_gaze_dir` are read directly from the file columns and are **never
smoothed**.

Consequence: our moving-average and median filter settings have no effect on the velocity
values or sample-level classification when `tobii_gaze_dir` is the active velocity method.

**Empirical verification:**
Running our filter on `A1averageWindow20T30.tsv` (300 Hz, binocular, Tobii no-smooth GT):

| Filter variant | κ | Sac Recall | Classifications different from no-smooth |
|---------------|---|------------|----------------------------------------|
| No smoothing | 0.9888 | 99.82 % | — |
| Moving Average 9 | 0.9888 | 99.82 % | 0 samples |
| Median 9 | 0.9888 | 99.82 % | 0 samples |

All smoothing variants produce **identical output** to no-smoothing when using
`tobii_gaze_dir`. Peak attenuation = 0.0 % for all saccade events.

Tobii's MA-9 setting **increases** saccade count by 1,007 relative to the no-smooth
baseline.  This is counter-intuitive if MA is understood as a low-pass blur that
attenuates velocity peaks.

Our reconstruction produces the same output regardless of smoothing setting, so our
filter's effective comparison for the MA-9 benchmark is:
**our no-smooth output vs Tobii's MA-9 GT**.

## Disagreement Analysis (Our Filter vs Tobii MA-9 GT)

Total disagreements on `A1averageWindow20T30SmoothAvg9.tsv`: 1,672 (3.69 %).

| Disagreement type | Count |
|------------------|-------|
| GT=Saccade → Pred=Fixation (missed saccades) | 1,283 |
| GT=Fixation → Pred=Saccade (false saccades) | 389 |

The 1,283 missed saccades have:
- Mean velocity in our filter: 14.1 °/s
- All 1,283 have velocity < 30 °/s in our filter
- Our filter correctly applies the threshold, but Tobii's MA-9 GT labels them as Saccade

This means: Tobii's MA-9 setting produces 1,283 saccade labels for samples that our
no-smooth filter does not classify as saccades.  These are samples that our filter
(independently of smoothing) classifies as Fixation (velocity < 30 °/s).

**The 81 % saccade recall figure does not reflect saccade peak attenuation by our MA.**
It reflects the count of Tobii-MA-9-saccades that also appear in our no-smooth output.

## Conclusion

**Hypothesis 1: Peak attenuation by our MA causes saccade miss rate**

**Evidence: None.** Our MA-9 smoothing has zero effect on velocity or classification.
Peak attenuation = 0 % for all 604 GT-saccade events.

**Hypothesis 2: Implementation gap — smoothing not applied to velocity-relevant data channel**

**Evidence: Strong.** The `tobii_gaze_dir` velocity method uses raw DACS direction vectors
that bypass the noise reduction stage.  Every smoothing variant produces output identical
to no-smoothing (0 classification differences, confirmed empirically).

**Hypothesis 3: Tobii's "Moving Average" operates on a different stage of the pipeline**

**Evidence: Consistent, but not directly verifiable.**

Tobii's MA-9 setting increases saccade count (+1,007 vs no-smooth baseline), which is
inconsistent with amplitude-reducing pre-smoothing.  This suggests Tobii's noise
reduction either:
(a) is applied to velocity values (post-velocity-computation), which could increase
    effective velocity sensitivity for gradual-onset saccades, or
(b) refers to a different algorithmic stage than gaze-coordinate pre-smoothing.

The exact Tobii mechanism is unknown (the whitepaper does not specify the stage at which
noise reduction is applied).

**Summary for thesis:**

The 81 % saccade recall reported for MA-9 in Table 11 reflects an implementation gap,
not a saccade-attenuation effect.  Our noise reduction stage smooths gaze screen
coordinates, but the `tobii_gaze_dir` velocity method reads raw direction vectors that
are never smoothed.  Consequently, any smoothing setting is a no-op in our pipeline, and
the disagreement with Tobii's MA-9 GT arises because Tobii's internal noise reduction
changes classification through an unresolved mechanism that increases saccade detection.
Disabling noise reduction in the reconstruction does not affect the output, confirming
that the reported degradation measures the distance between our no-smooth filter and
Tobii's MA-9 filter — not the effect of our own smoothing implementation.

Tobii's internal noise reduction produces these additional saccades is not specified in the
whitepaper and remains an unresolved reconstruction gap.  Routing the direction-vector
pipeline through the smoothing stage is the most direct path to resolving this gap in
future work.
